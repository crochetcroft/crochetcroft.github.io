The MLSE Study

Development of a multilingual screening test for language ability in progressive aphasia

This is a multi-centre study based at St George's Hospital, University of London, conducted in conjunction with researchers at the University of Cambridge, and the University of Manchester. I am currently working as a Research Associate for the Cambridge arm of the MLSE study. The study aims to develop a brief linguistic assessment tool to classify progressive aphasic syndromes. As part of this project, I will use the MLSE to characterise language impairment in other types of dementia not usually associated with changes in language.

NPH Research

Cognition and apathy in normal pressure hydrocephalus (NPH)

This research was conducted as part of my PhD in the Department of Psychiatry at the University of Cambridge. I investigated cognitive outcome following shunt treatment for NPH, and the significance of the symptom of apathy for cognition and outcome.
