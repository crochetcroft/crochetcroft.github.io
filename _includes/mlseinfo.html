The MLSE study is an MRC-funded, multi-centre project which aims to develop a clinical test for the assessment and monitoring of progressive aphasic syndromes: The Mini Linguistic State Examination (MLSE). I am a research associate for the Cambridge arm of the MLSE study. We are currently recruiting patients with primary progressive aphasia to take part in the MLSE study in Cambridge, London, and Manchester. We are also recruiting patients with other types of dementia, and patients with post-stroke aphasia. We aim to begin publishing various papers resulting from the MLSE study in the coming months.
